## 1. Night Before Brew Day – Water & Prep

**Water Additions**
- Mash Water: 15.88 L
- Sparge Water: 9.62 L

**Water Chemistry**
- Add 4 mL lactic acid (88%) to mash water
- Add 2 mL lactic acid (88%) to sparge water

**Grain Bill (Total: 4.18 kg)**
- 3.73 kg Maris Otter
- 0.22 kg Crystal Maple / Carapils
- 0.15 kg Crystal Light
- 0.08 kg Pale Crystal Malt

---

## 2. Brew Day – Mashing

- Heat mash water to 70–71°C

**Mash**
- Target: 67°C
- Time: 60 min
- Stir thoroughly at mash-in (no dry pockets)
- Re-check temperature after 5–10 minutes and adjust if needed

**Mash Out**
- Raise to 75°C for 10 minutes

---

## 3. Sparging

- Sparge Temp: 78–80°C
- Target Pre-Boil Volume: 22.21 L
- Target Pre-Boil Gravity: ~1.050

**Adjustments if needed:**
- If gravity is low → extend boil time
- If volume is low → small top-up

---

## 4. Boiling (60 Minutes)

**Hop Additions**
- 60 min – 10 g Citra (12%)
- 15 min – 12 g Cascade (5.5%)
- 15 min – 12 g Citra (12%)

**Whirlpool / Hop Stand**
- Cool wort to ~75–78°C before adding hops
- 20 min – 12 g Cascade + 12 g Citra

---

## 5. Cooling & Transfer

- Chill to: ~20–23°C
- OG Target: 1.062

**Transfer**
- BrewZilla → liquid disconnect → fermenting keg
- Fill through liquid post
- Avoid splashing during transfer

**After transfer**
- Gently shake or swirl keg for 30–60 seconds to introduce oxygen

---

## 6. Fermentation (Keg)

- Yeast: California Ale (WLP001)
- Fermentation Temp: 20°C

**Early Fermentation**
- Blow-off tube (first 48 hours)
- No pressure during early fermentation

**Pressure Fermentation**
- After ~48 hours or near end of active fermentation
- Switch to spunding valve
- Pressure: 10–12 PSI

**Dry Hop (Day 4)**
- Depressurize keg before opening
- 12 g Cascade
- 12 g Citra

**After adding hops**
- Seal keg
- Purge headspace with CO₂ 2–3 times

**Fermentation Completion**
- Duration: ~10–14 days

---

## 7. Cold Crash

- Temp: ~0°C
- Duration: 2–5 days
- Maintain 5–10 PSI during cold crash

---

## 8. Closed Transfer

- Pressure: 2–5 PSI
- CO₂ push transfer
- Spunding valve on receiving keg

---

## 9. Carbonation & Serving

- Target: 2.4 volumes CO₂
- Serving Pressure: ~10–12 PSI

---

## 10. Notes

- No hop spider – loose hops used
- Whirlpool improves hop aroma extraction
- Closed transfer minimizes oxygen exposure
- Keg fermentation with pressure control
- Monitor pre-boil gravity to stay on target
